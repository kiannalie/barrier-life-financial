What is Final Expense Insurance?

Final expense insurance — also called burial insurance or funeral insurance — is a type of whole life insurance policy with a smaller face value (typically $5,000–$50,000) designed specifically to cover end-of-life costs. These include funeral and burial expenses, medical bills, outstanding debts, and any other costs your family may face after your passing.

Because the coverage amounts are smaller and underwriting is simplified, final expense insurance is one of the easiest types of life insurance to qualify for — especially for seniors or those with health conditions who may not qualify for traditional life insurance.

How Final Expense Insurance Works

Final expense insurance works like any whole life policy — you pay a fixed monthly premium, and when you pass away, your beneficiaries receive a tax-free death benefit. The key differences are:

  • Smaller coverage amounts — typically $5,000 to $50,000
  • No medical exam required — approval is based on a few health questions or guaranteed regardless of health
  • Permanent coverage — it never expires and premiums never increase
  • Builds cash value — like all whole life policies, it accumulates a small cash value over time

Simplified Issue vs. Guaranteed Issue

Simplified issue policies ask a short list of health questions (no exam) and typically offer better rates. Guaranteed issue policies accept anyone regardless of health — no questions asked — but have a 2-year waiting period before the full death benefit is available.

Who Needs Final Expense Insurance & Why?

Final expense insurance is especially valuable for:

  • Adults aged 50–85 who want to ensure their final costs are covered
  • People who don't qualify for traditional life insurance due to age or health conditions
  • Individuals with fixed or limited incomes who need affordable permanent coverage
  • Anyone who wants to spare their family from scrambling to cover funeral costs
  • Those who have outstanding medical bills or small debts they don't want to leave behind

Common Use Cases for Final Expense Coverage

The average funeral in the United States costs between $7,000 and $12,000. Without life insurance, that cost often falls entirely on surviving family members. Final expense coverage can be used for:

  • Funeral home services and burial or cremation
  • Casket, urn, or cemetery plot fees
  • Headstone or memorial marker
  • Outstanding medical or hospice bills
  • Credit card balances or small personal loans
  • Travel expenses for out-of-town family
  • Any other costs the family chooses to use it for

Typical Policy Features

Coverage Amounts

Most final expense policies offer between $5,000 and $50,000 in coverage. Some carriers go up to $100,000 for healthier applicants.

Age Ranges

Policies are typically available to applicants between ages 45 and 85. Some carriers extend to age 89 for guaranteed issue policies.

Waiting Periods

Simplified issue policies typically have no waiting period — full coverage begins immediately. Guaranteed issue policies often have a 2-year graded benefit period, during which only premiums paid plus interest are returned if death occurs.

Premiums

Premiums are fixed for life — they will never go up, and the policy will never be cancelled due to age or health changes as long as you pay premiums.

Key Benefits of Final Expense Insurance

Affordability & Fixed Premiums

Because coverage amounts are smaller, premiums are much lower than a traditional whole life policy. A healthy 65-year-old may pay as little as $40–$60/month for $10,000 in coverage.

No Medical Exam / Simplified Underwriting

Most final expense policies don't require a physical exam. You answer a short health questionnaire, or in the case of guaranteed issue, no questions at all.

Instant Approval and Peace of Mind

With simplified underwriting and instant decisions, you can get covered today — giving you and your family peace of mind immediately.

Builds Cash Value

Like all whole life policies, final expense coverage grows a cash value over time that you can borrow against if needed.

Pros and Cons of Final Expense Insurance

Pros

  • No medical exam required
  • Guaranteed issue options available
  • Affordable monthly premiums
  • Permanent coverage — never expires
  • Premiums never increase
  • Builds small cash value
  • Fast approval — often same day

Cons

  • Lower coverage limits than standard life insurance
  • Higher cost per dollar of coverage vs. term
  • Graded benefit period for guaranteed issue
  • Not ideal for income replacement

Sample Scenario

Maria is 68 years old and recently retired. She has a fixed income and doesn't want to burden her adult children with funeral costs when she passes. She applies for a simplified issue final expense policy with $15,000 in coverage for approximately $58/month.

She names her daughter as beneficiary. When Maria passes away several years later, her daughter receives the $15,000 death benefit within days — tax-free — and uses it to cover the funeral, a small medical bill, and a family gathering in Maria's memory. Her children never had to worry about coming up with the money out of pocket.

Is Final Expense Coverage a Good Choice?

If you're over 50 and want to make sure your final expenses don't become your family's financial burden — yes. Final expense insurance is one of the most compassionate financial decisions you can make for your loved ones.

It's not the most cost-efficient insurance per dollar of coverage, but that's not the point. It's designed to be accessible to everyone, regardless of age or health, and to provide a simple, guaranteed way to take care of your family's immediate needs after you're gone.

If you're younger and primarily need income replacement, a term life policy will give you more coverage for a lower premium. But for seniors focused on end-of-life costs, final expense insurance is the right fit.
